Radical Therapy Primer

Why AI Made Agile Obsolete

Agile was designed for a world where humans wrote every line of code, manually coordinated every handoff, and planned in 2-week sprints. But AI changed everything. This primer explains why your Agile process is now the bottleneck‚Äîand what comes next.

Section 1: Why Frameworks Fail

Process Theater vs. Real Work

Your team spends 5-10 hours per week in sprint planning, standups, and retros. Meanwhile, GitHub Copilot writes code faster than you can plan it. The coordination overhead that Agile was designed to manage is now automated by AI.

The paradox: You adopted AI tools to move faster, but you're still planning like it's 2015. Sprint commitments, story point estimation, and manual status updates feel like process theater‚Äîperforming productivity rather than creating value.

The Illusion of Predictability

Agile promised predictability through sprint commitments. But AI-powered development is inherently unpredictable. When Copilot completes a feature in 2 hours instead of 2 days, your sprint plan is instantly obsolete.

The reality: You can't predict velocity when AI writes half your code. Instead of fighting this unpredictability, RAD Process embraces it through continuous adaptive planning.

Why Compliance Kills Creativity

Agile became a checklist: daily standups ‚úì, sprint retrospectives ‚úì, burndown charts ‚úì. Teams follow the rituals but lose the principles. Compliance replaces judgment.

The cost: Engineers spend more time updating Jira than shipping code. Product managers become sprint coordinators instead of strategic thinkers. Creativity dies under the weight of process compliance.

Section 2: The Three Foundations of Radical Therapy

1. Flow Over Frameworks

What flow means for product teams: Uninterrupted time for deep work. Minimal context switching. Continuous shipping instead of sprint-based releases.

How to measure it: Not story points. Not velocity. Instead: time to ship, developer satisfaction, and actual outcomes delivered.

Common flow killers: Daily standups that break focus. Sprint planning marathons. Manual status updates. Jira ticket hygiene. Context switching between "work" and "process."

2. Human-in-the-Loop

AI as amplifier, not replacement: AI writes code, but humans decide what to build. AI surfaces insights, but humans make strategic calls. AI automates coordination, but humans maintain relationships.

Where humans add the most value: Defining outcomes. Prioritizing trade-offs. Maintaining vision. Building trust. Making judgment calls that AI can't.

How to integrate AI without losing judgment: Let AI handle coordination overhead. Let humans focus on strategy, creativity, and relationships. Use AI to amplify human decision-making, not replace it.

3. Radical Transparency

Why transparency is uncomfortable (and necessary): When everyone sees the same truth, there's nowhere to hide. Misalignment becomes visible. Progress (or lack of it) is undeniable. Uncomfortable? Yes. Essential? Absolutely.

The difference between visibility and surveillance: Surveillance tracks activity. Transparency reveals outcomes. Surveillance measures hours. Transparency measures impact.

Building trust through truth: When AI generates real-time insights about what's working and what's not, teams can't hide behind Jira tickets. They have to confront reality‚Äîand that builds trust faster than any retro.

Section 3: Principles in Practice

How Radical Therapy Changes Planning

Instead of sprint planning, use AI-assisted adaptive planning. Define outcomes, not features. Let AI map dependencies and risks. Prioritize continuously, not every 2 weeks.

Example: Instead of "5 story points for user auth," try "Users can securely log in using OAuth." AI helps break it down. Humans decide if it's the right priority.

How It Transforms Standups and Retros

Standups become optional. AI-generated status updates replace manual check-ins. Only meet when human coordination is needed‚Äînot by default.

Retros become real-time. AI surfaces blockers, velocity changes, and process friction continuously. No need to wait 2 weeks to learn what's broken.

How It Changes Roadmaps

Roadmaps become living documents, not static plans. AI adapts priorities based on velocity, dependencies, and business needs. Teams ship continuously instead of waiting for sprint boundaries.

Result: Faster shipping, less process overhead, more strategic thinking.
