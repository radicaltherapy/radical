RAD Process Planning Guide

Replace Sprints with AI-Adaptive Delivery

What Is RAD Process Planning?

RAD Process (Rapid Adaptive Development) planning replaces fixed sprint commitments with AI-assisted, continuously adaptive delivery.

Instead of estimating story points and committing to 2-week sprints, you let AI help you plan based on real-time velocity, changing priorities, and actual outcomes.

The Core Shift:

Agile asks: "What can we commit to shipping in 2 weeks?"
RAD Process asks: "What outcome do we want to create, and how can AI help us get there fastest?"

The 5-Step RAD Process Workflow

1

Define Outcomes, Not Features

What change do you want to create? What does success look like? AI can help you break down outcomes into shippable increments, but humans define what success means.

Example:

3

Adaptive Prioritization

Priorities change. RAD Process embraces this. Instead of locking in sprint commitments, use AI to continuously reprioritize based on real conditions.

AI prioritizes based on:

  • Business value shifts: Customer feedback changes what matters most
  • Technical discoveries: A dependency breaks or a simpler path emerges
  • Team velocity changes: AI completed a task faster than expected
  • External dependencies: A third-party API becomes available (or breaks)

The mental shift:

Sprint commitments punish adaptation. RAD Process rewards it. When conditions change, you adjust‚Äîwithout guilt or lengthy replanning meetings.

4

Maintain Flow, Not Sprint Cadence

Stop forcing work into 2-week boxes. RAD Process optimizes for continuous flow. Ship when ready, not when the sprint ends.

How to measure flow:

Track cycle time (idea to production), developer satisfaction, and defect rates. Ignore story points and sprint velocity‚Äîthey measure compliance, not outcomes.
